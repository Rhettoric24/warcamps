UNIT ANALYSIS & BALANCE NOTES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

✓ BALANCED UNITS:
  • Spearmen (10 S): Perfect cost-to-power ratio. Good starting unit. 75% survival 
    provides reasonable durability. Standard infantry role well-defined.
  
  • Archers (15 S): Premium infantry (higher survival than Spearmen). 15 S cost 
    appears justified for 90% survival vs 75%. Good specialization example.

⚠ QUESTIONABLE BALANCE:

  1. BRIDGECREWS (5 S) - POSSIBLE ISSUE:
     - Cost-to-power ratio: 50.0 (vs Spearmen's 10.0)
     - Design: Trash unit that's 2x more expensive per power than Spearmen
     - Problem: No compelling reason to use these. Spearmen are straight superior.
     - Recommendation: Either (a) reduce to 2-3 S to make them "filler for poor players,"
       or (b) remove entirely if they serve no strategic purpose.
     
  2. CHULLS (50 S) - TRANSPORT COST UNCLEAR:
     - 0.5 power + 10 carry + -0.025 speed penalty for 50 S
     - Cost is 5x a Spearman (10 S vs 50 S)
     - Issue: Does 10 carry capacity justify 5x cost? Depends on mission reward scaling.
     - Also has -0.025 speed penalty (slows army down)
     - Recommendation: Review mission economy - do carry units yield rewards that justify cost?
       If no high-carry missions exist, carry capacity may be wasted stat.
  
  3. SHARDBEARERS (1 Gemheart) - HIGH GATING:
     - 0 base power but 2.0x multiplier on other units
     - Cost: 1 Gemheart = 10,000 S at black market rate
     - Problem: Gemshearts are SCARCEST resource (from raids 10%, plateau runs, exchange)
     - Issue: Is 2.0x multiplier powerful enough to justify *only* Gemheart cost? 
       It only affects other shardbearers in same deployment, so stacking required.
     - Recommendation: Consider if Shardbearers should scale with other unit types too,
       or if their multiplier stacking mechanic is too niche.

✗ ESPIONAGE UNITS - SEPARATE ECONOMY:
  
  • Noble (50 S): 1 power, 50% survival - LOWEST survival ratio in game
    - Same cost as Chull but much frailer
    - Only 50% survival vs Chull's 80%
    - Recommendation: Either increase Noble survival to 65%+ or reduce cost to 30 S
  
  • Spy Agent (150 S): 2 power, 70% survival - reasonable middle option
    
  • Ghostblood (600 S): 5 power, 90% survival - premium but only for espionage
    - Cost per power: 120 S/power (same as Ghostblood regular combat, 600÷5=120)
    - This is expensive compared to Archers (15÷1=15), but justified by rarity/specialization

UNIT BALANCE SUMMARY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
✓ Well-designed: Spearmen, Archers, Ghostblood
⚠ Needs review: Bridgecrews (purpose unclear), Chulls (carry economy?), 
                 Shardbearers (multiplier only applies to other shardbearers)
✗ Fragile: Noble spies (50% survival is very low)

BUILDING-BY-BUILDING ANALYSIS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

INCOME BUILDINGS:
─────────────────
✓ MARKET (250 S, +400 S/day):
  - ROI: 250 S payback in 0.6 days (~14 hours in dev mode with 20-second days)
  - One of the fastest payback buildings in the game
  - Recommendatio n: WELL-BALANCED. Should be purchased constantly.

✓ SOULCASTER (150 S, +50 provision):
  - ROI: Depends on provision usage (army size scaling)
  - Cheaper than Market but harder to measure value (indirect)
  - If each provision supports ~1 unit, +50 provisions = capacity for more income/military
  - Recommendation: WELL-BALANCED for early game. More valuable with large armies.

MILITARY BUILDINGS:
───────────────────
⚠ TRAINING CAMP (7,000 S, +10% power):
  - Cost: High (28x a Market building)
  - Effect: Additive 10% to ALL unitspower (stacks with other camps)
  - Problem: Expensive for what seems like modest returns
  - Harsh scaling: 5 land cost makes it land-intensive
  - Recommendation: Consider if +10% power is meaningful enough at 7,000 S cost.
    Compare: 5 Markets (1,250 S) give income that can buy 700 cheap units. Which is better?

⚠ MONASTERY (7,000 S, +5% survival):
  - Cost: Same as Training Camp (high)
  - Effect: +5% to ALL unit survival (stacks with others)
  - Problem: Even more subtle benefit than Training Camp
  - Also has 50 S/day upkeep (ongoing cost)
  - Recommendation: Consider if +5% survival is valuable enough. Also costs 50 S/day upkeep
    making long-term cost much higher than Training Camp.

INFRASTRUCTURE BUILDINGS:
──────────────────────────
✓ STORMSHELTER (500 S, protect 1 building, 25 S/day upkeep):
  - Cost: Moderate (2x Market)
  - Effect: Protects 1 building from highstorm destruction (5% chance per protected building)
  - Upkeep: 25 S/day (~1% of a Market's daily income)
  - Recommendation: REASONABLE. Scales with how many valuable buildings you have.
    Players need to judge: Is a Market+ Soulcaster worth protecting from 5% destruction risk?

GATING BUILDINGS (Unlock new systems):
───────────────────────────────────────
✓ SPY NETWORK (5,000 S, unlock espionage, max 1, 75 S/day upkeep):
  - Cost: Moderate for gatekeeping a whole system
  - Effect: Enables entire espionage subsystem (sabotage, intelligence gathering)
  - Upkeep: 75 S/day ongoing cost
  - Recommendation: WELL-DESIGNED. Meaningful gate on powerful system.

✓ RESEARCH LIBRARY (10,000 S, unlock fabrials, max 1):
  - Cost: Highest non-Whisper building (40x Market)
  - Effect: Enables entire fabrial system (multipliers, arena abilities)
  - No upkeep!
  - Recommendation: WELL-DESIGNED. Heavy cost justified - fabrials are powerful.

✗ WHISPER TOWER (10 Ghostbloods, ultimate spy, max 1):
  - Cost: 10 Ghostbloods = 6,000 S equivalent (extremely expensive)
  - Effect: Unknown in stats sheet, described as "Ultimate spy defense & offense"
  - Problem: NO UPKEEP COST despite being ultra-expensive and single-instance
  - Recommendation: UNCLEAR without knowing exact effect. If it provides huge espionage 
    bonus, may be balanced. Need to clarify mechanics vs cost.

BUILDING BALANCE SUMMARY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
✓ Well-designed: Market, Soulcaster, Stormshelter, Spy Network, Research Library
⚠ Needs clarification: Training Camp (high cost, modest effect?)
⚠ Needs clarification: Monastery (high cost + upkeep, only +5% survival?)
✗ Needs review: Whisper Tower (mechanics unclear relative to 6,000 S cost)

FABRIAL ANALYSIS & BALANCE NOTES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

✓ MISSION FABRIALS - COST-BENEFIT ANALYSIS:

  • HEATRIAL (3 GH, 1.5x provision cap):
    - Cost: 3 Gemhearts (rare - only from raids/plateau/exchange)
    - Effect: +50% provision capacity (stack-able)
    - Benefit: More soldiers deployed = more power in missions
    - Recommendation: REASONABLE but expensive. Only worth if military is army-capped.

  • LEDGER (3 GH, 1.5x market income):
    - Cost: 3 Gemhearts (same as Heatrial)
    - Effect: +50% income from all Markets
    - With 1 Market alone: 400 S/day → 600 S/day = +200 S/day
    - ROI example: 3 Gemhearts = 30,000 S at exchange rate
    - Payback: 30,000 ÷ 200 = 150 days to recover cost
    - ⚠ PROBLEM: Takes 5 months to pay for itself! Too expensive for passive income.
    - Recommendation: Either reduce cost to 1-2 Gemhearts or increase multiplier to 2.0x.

  • GRAVITY LIFT (5 GH, 2.0x mission speed):
    - Cost: 5 Gemhearts (most expensive regular fabrial)
    - Effect: 2x on ALL mission speeds (finish missions in half time)
    - Strategic impact: Missions→ more missions/day → more sphere income
    - Indirect benefit: Massive efficiency boost
    - Recommendation: BALANCED. High cost justified by time-compression benefit.

✗ ARENA FABRIALS - SEPARATE ECONOMY:

  • REGEN PLATE (1 GH, heal champion daily):
    - Cost: Only 1 Gemheart (cheapest fabrial!)
    - Effect: Full heal once per day (powerful in arena grinding)
    - Recommendation: POSSIBLY UNDERPRICED. 1 GH is cheap for daily healing.

  • THRILL AMP (2 GH, +2 Thrill, -1 HP per win):
    - Cost: 2 Gemhearts (moderate)
    - Effect: Trade-off mechanic (buff power, hurt durability)
    - Usefulness: Only useful if Thrill mechanic is important in arena
    - Recommendation: DEPENDS on arena economy - need to know if Thrill is valuable.

  • HALF-SHARD (4 GH, 0.5x damage reduction):
    - Cost: 4 Gemhearts (expensive)
    - Effect: Halve incoming damage in arena (massive durability buff)
    - Recommendation: EXPENSIVE but powerful. 4 GH seems high unless damage spikes are severe.

FABRIAL BALANCE SUMMARY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
✓ Well-designed: Heatrial, Gravity Lift
⚠ Needs rebalancing: Ledger (ROI too long - takes 150 days to pay back)
⚠ Needs clarification: Arena Fabrials (depend on unknown arena economy dynamics)
✗ Possibly underpriced: Regen Plate (1 GH for daily healing seems cheap)

SECTION 4: RESOURCE ECONOMY BALANCE REVIEW
================================================================================

RESOURCE FLOW RATES (Dev Mode - 10 second days):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Starting Resources:
  • Spheres: 25,000 S (dev)
  • Gemhearts: 10 GH (dev)
  • Provisions: 150
  • Territory: 0/50

Daily Income Sources:
  • Base market income: 0 S/day (must build Market = +400 S/day each)
  • Gemheart sources: 10% of attack raids ONLY, plateau runs (coalition), exchange 10 GH = 10,000 S
  • Provision sources: +50 per Soulcaster Bunker (expires with death/mission)

GEMHEART SCARCITY ANALYSIS:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Problem: Gemhearts are 10x scarcer than Spheres
  - Only 3 obtainable ways: Raid drops (10% chance), Plateau runs, Market exchange (10,000 S)
  - All fabrials require 1-5 Gemhearts
  - All high-tier spy units require Gemhearts (Shardbearers = 1 GH each)
  - All arena fabrials require Gemhearts

Implication: Players will face "GH bottleneck" around mid-game
  - Can't afford everything that costs Gemhearts
  - Must choose between: fabrials vs. Shardbearers vs. arena equipment

Recommendation: CHECK that this is intentional gating design.
  If you want Gemhearts scarce for challenge: STATUS QUO is good.
  If you want Gemhearts more accessible: Consider increasing raid drop to 15% or
  adding more plateau run frequency.

SPHERE ECONOMY ANALYSIS:
────────────────────────
Income sources (Dev mode testing):
  • Market: 400 S/day per Market (easy to scale)
  • Scout mission: 500-1,500 S (1 day mission)
  • Attack mission: 2,000-5,000 S (1.5 day mission)
  • Conquest mission: Returns cost only, uses 200 S + 100 provision as cost
  • Plateau run: Shared loot, variable (2 day mission)

Spending sinks:
  • Unit recruitment: 5-600 S per unit (scaling)
  • Building construction: 150-10,000 S (one-time per type)
  • Upkeep: 25-75 S/day per building
  • Conquest cost: 200 S + 100 provision per conquest attempt

Assessment: Sphere income feels abundant for mid-game (Markets scale well)
  Recommendation: BALANCED. Players should have constant sphere income to spend.


PROVISION ECONOMY ANALYSIS:
────────────────────────────
Starting: 150 provisions
  • Each unit costs 1-3 provisions per recruitment (one-time)
  • Army size determines provision "cap" (200-unit cap=200 provisions used)
  • Soulcasters increase cap: +50 per bunker
  • Heatrial multiplier: 1.5x cap total

Problem: Unclear if provisions gate army size or just add flavor cost
  - If provisions are HARD CAP on army: This matters a lot (balance critical)
  - If provisions are SOFT mechanic: Less critical but still important

Recommendation: CLARIFY in UI whether provisions are:
  • Hard cap (can't recruit more units if at provision limit)
  • Soft penalty (upkeep cost increase if over cap)
  Either way, current design works but needs clearer communication.


TERRITORY ECONOMY ANALYSIS:
───────────────────────────
Starting: 0/50 territory
  • Each conquest: +10-25 territory per success
  • Land cost: Buildings require 1-5 land each
  • Max capacity: 50 territory

Problem: Land seems abundant (50 total allows ~8-10 buildings)
  - Most players won't hit territory cap
  - Conquest feels like "endgame expansion" not "early-game bottleneck"

Recommendation: Either:
  • Increase building land costs (make land more contested)
  • Decrease territory rewards per conquest (make expansion slower)
  • Increase territory cap (if abundance is intentional)
  As-is: Territory cap is not a binding constraint for most players.

SECTION 5: OVERALL BALANCE RECOMMENDATIONS (Priority Order)

1. [CRITICAL] Ledger Fabrial Rebalance

     a) Reduce to 1-2 GH (now 50-100 day payback)
     b) Increase to 2.0x multiplier (now 100 day payback)

2. [CRITICAL] Bridgecrews Purpose Clarification
   Current: 5 S, 0.1 power, 25% survival (worse than Spearmen in every way)
   Question: Are these intentional "newbie trap" unit?
   Options:
     a) Keep but reduce to 2-3 S (makes them true "filler" unit)
     b) Remove entirely (simplify unit roster)
     c) Give them unique bonus (e.g., "Cost 50% less upkeep" or "Double carry capacity")

3. [CRITICAL] Whisper Tower Mechanics Clarification
   Current: 10 Ghostbloods cost (6,000 S equivalent), effect unknown
   Need info: What exactly does this building do? If it's powerful, cost may be justified.
   If effect is modest, cost is too high.

─────────────────────────────────

4. [IMPORTANT] Training Camp & Monastery Cost Review
   Current: Both 7,000 S, similar value proposition
   Problem: Expensive for +10% power and +5% survival (feel passive, not exciting)
   Recommendation: Consider if these should be cheaper or have more impactful effects
   Compare against: 28x Market costs only 1,250 S total

5. [IMPORTANT] Noble Spy Survival Buff
   Current: 50% survival (lowest in game)

   Recommendation: Increase to 65-70% survival or reduce cost to 30 S

6. [IMPORTANT] Arena Fabrial Economy Validation
   Current: Regen Plate (1 GH), Thrill Amp (2 GH), Half-Shard (4 GH)

   Current: Mechanic exists but function unclear
   Recommendation: Decide if provisions are hard cap or soft penalty, then
   communicate clearly in UI

   Current: Very scarce (intentional design), may frustrate casual players
